Systemic Friction Points

Anatomy of Delay

Cardiac emergencies demand seamless coordination, but systemic fragmentation transforms the golden hour into a series of preventable waiting periods. Here's where time hemorrhages.

Time Loss
Broken Link

Current Emergency Workflow Constriction Points

01

Sequential Dependency Trap

Each step waits for the previous step to complete. ECG capture must finish before interpretation begins. Interpretation must conclude before the specialist call is made. No parallelizationevery delay compounds into the next.

02

Fragmented Data Silos

Ambulances, PHCs, tertiary hospitals, e-Hospital, and STEMI app operate as isolated islands. Patient data captured in the ambulance doesn't flow to hospital dashboards. Systems don't speak to each otherrequiring manual re-entry at each handoff.

03

Signal Integrity Bottleneck

Patient motion, baseline wander, and inconsistent lead placement degrade ECG quality in moving ambulances. Without automated artifact detection or guided lead verification, paramedics must re-capture signalscreating queues and risking false alerts.

04

Specialist Interpretation Queue

Limited cardiologists must manually review every ECG reading. When multiple emergencies occur simultaneously, ECGs stack up waiting for specialist attention. Paramedics lack diagnostic authority, creating a single point of failure for the entire workflow.

05

Rural Connectivity Void

Significant portions of Andhra Pradesh have intermittent or no cellular connectivity. Without offline caching or store-and-forward mechanisms, ECG data captured in rural areas cannot be transmitted until connectivity resumesif it resumes in time.

06

Capacity & Routing Mismatch

Ambulances route to the nearest hospital, not the nearest equipped or available cath lab. Without real-time capacity signals from hospital networks, rerouting occurs at the gatewasting critical minutes in ping-pong between facilities.

07

Blind Arrival Syndrome

Hospitals receive no advance warning about incoming cardiac emergencies. One-way communication and absence of ETA plus telemetry pre-alerts prevent hospitals from preparing cath labs in parallel. Only upon ambulance arrival does the scramble begin.

08

Single-Path Alert Delivery

Critical alerts depend on a single network route with no multi-path failover. When connectivity drops or services fail, messages are lost with no store-and-forward backup. A single SIM or network path becomes a catastrophic single point of failure.

09

Consent & Compliance Gate

In emergencies, ambiguity around consent and data sharing stalls transmission to hospitals. Lack of codified implied-consent workflows under DPDP Act requirements leads to hesitancyparamedics and systems await manual approvals that delay critical data transfer.

10

Device Heterogeneity

Ambulance fleets across Andhra Pradesh use ECG devices from multiple manufacturers with proprietary data formats. Inconsistent use of HL7/FHIR/MQTT and proprietary 12-lead schemas blocks standardized ingestionadapters are ad-hoc and fragile.

These bottlenecks compound into one systemic failure

The Compounding Effect

No single bottleneck operates in isolation. Sequential workflows multiply delays. Information silos prevent coordination. Connectivity gaps lose critical data. Consent ambiguity creates hesitation. Device incompatibility blocks integration. Together, they transform what should be a streamlined emergency response into a fragmented series of preventable waiting periodseach consuming irreplaceable minutes from the golden hour.

Where fragmented systems once created delays, AI now weaves a unified response fabricconnecting ambulances, clinicians, and hospitals into a single, synchronized cardiac care network.

Heart Smart Grid

AI-Integrated Cardiac Emergency Response

For 108 ambulances, PHCs, and tertiary hospitals across Andhra Pradesh

Edge AI Diagnosis

Instant STEMI and arrhythmia detection on ambulance tabletsno specialist wait, no connectivity required. Clinical-grade accuracy in under 5 seconds, with multi-model cloud validation when online.

Heals Bottleneck 04: Specialist Queue & Bottleneck 01: Sequential Dependency

Signal Quality & Lead Guidance

Detects motion artifacts, baseline wander, and lead reversal in real time. Visual guidance helps paramedics correct electrode placement before analysiseliminating recapture delays.

Heals Bottleneck 03: Signal Integrity

Unified Data Fabric

Standard adapters for HL7/FHIR/MQTT normalize ECG ingestion from BPL, Schiller, and Philips devices. Data captured once flows everywhere instantly via secure backend APIs.

Heals Bottleneck 02: Data Silos & Bottleneck 10: Device Heterogeneity

Smart Hospital Pre-Alerting

Cath labs prepared before ambulance arrives. Routes to nearest PCI-capable facility based on real-time availability, not just distance. Multi-channel alerts via push, SMS, and voice ensure no notification is missed.

Heals Bottleneck 07: Blind Arrival & Bottleneck 06: Capacity Mismatch

Multi-Path Alert Failover

Redundant delivery via dashboard, push, SMS, and voice with automatic retries and acknowledgment tracking. Dead letter queues ensure zero critical alerts are ever lostno single point of failure.

Heals Bottleneck 08: Single-Path Alert Delivery

Offline-First Design

Store-and-forward via MQTT ensures zero data loss in rural dead zones. AI diagnosis continues locally on edge; data syncs automatically when signal returns. Binary SMS fallback for extreme conditions.
