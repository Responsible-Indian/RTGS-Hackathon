Problem Landscape

The Accountability Void in In-Plant Training

Manual attendance tracking at industry partner sites creates a cascade of verification failures‚Äîleaving student presence unconfirmed, training quality unmeasured, and stakeholders without reliable oversight.

01

üìù Unverifiable Paper Trails

Attendance at industry locations is recorded on manual registers or spreadsheets that can be easily altered, lost, or backdated. Without digital verification, there's no way to confirm authenticity.

High fraud vulnerability
02

üìç No Location Verification

Supervisors have no reliable way to confirm whether students were physically present at the assigned industry location. A signed register proves nothing about actual whereabouts.

Location blind spot
03

üë• The Proxy Attendance Loophole

Without biometric or face recognition verification, students can easily have peers or others sign attendance on their behalf. This fundamental vulnerability undermines the entire purpose of mandatory in-plant training‚Äîstudents may receive credit for training they never attended, graduating with skills they never acquired.

Skills certification at risk
04

üîå Disconnected Data Silos

Partial attendance data exists across the ITI portal, industry records, and coordinator files‚Äîbut none of it is integrated. Without a centralized system, comprehensive monitoring remains impossible.

No unified oversight
05

‚õìÔ∏è Broken Accountability Chain

Without verifiable records, neither students nor industry partners can be held accountable for training attendance. When disputes arise, there's no reliable evidence to reference.

Unresolvable disputes

The Core Challenge

In-plant training is meant to bridge classroom learning and industry readiness. But when attendance verification fails, the entire foundation of vocational skill certification becomes unreliable‚Äîproducing graduates whose actual training experience remains unverified and unmeasured.

Root Cause Analysis

Where Verification Breaks Down

The problems persist not from lack of intent, but from structural constraints embedded in how attendance data flows‚Äîor fails to flow‚Äîacross a geographically dispersed, manually operated, and systemically fragmented ecosystem.

Bottleneck 01

Identity Not Bound to Attendance Event

At the moment of capture, a pen signature on paper has no cryptographic or biometric link to the student's verified identity. There is no face match, no device fingerprint, no timestamp server, and no location proof. This fundamental gap at the point of origin is why proxy attendance is structurally possible‚Äîanyone can sign any name, and the record carries no chain-of-custody proving who actually made the mark.

Proxy attendance structurally enabled
Face GPS Time Device
Bottleneck 02

Geographic Dispersion Without Location Binding

Students train at industry locations scattered across districts‚Äîfactories, workshops, and facilities with no digital connection to ITI headquarters. Attendance entries are not bound to coordinates or site geo-fences, so presence is unverifiable. Coordinators cannot physically visit each site daily, and there's no automated channel feeding location-verified data back in real time.

Location blind spot across all sites
Face GPS Time Device
Bottleneck 03

The Monthly Reporting Lag

Attendance data follows a slow cascade: recorded daily, compiled weekly, reported monthly. Intermittent or no connectivity at factory sites enforces offline capture and batch reporting. By the time irregularities surface in consolidated reports reaching ITI coordinators, the training period may already be half complete. This delay transforms every issue from preventable to historical‚Äîsomething to document, not something to address.

Intervention window closes before detection
Face GPS Time Device
Bottleneck 04

Human-to-Human Transfer Points

Every piece of attendance data passes through multiple human hands: student signs register, supervisor verifies, HR compiles, coordinator receives. Each handoff is a potential point of delay, alteration, or loss. Without digital chain-of-custody, there's no way to trace when or where data was modified‚Äîor whether it reflects what actually happened.

No audit trail across transfer points
Face GPS Time Device
Bottleneck 05

No Integration Infrastructure

The ITI portal, industry HR systems, and district dashboards were never designed to communicate. Each maintains its own data format, storage, and access patterns. Lack of shared identifiers (student/site IDs) and data standards prevents automated reconciliation. Even when partial attendance data exists in one system, there's no automated pathway to synchronize it elsewhere.

Single source of truth impossible
Face GPS Time Device
Bottleneck 06

Supervision Bandwidth Mismatch

Industry supervisors have production responsibilities‚Äîtraining oversight is secondary at best. KPIs prioritize output over attendance verification. With limited capacity to verify each student's presence meaningfully, they rely on students self-reporting via sign-in sheets. Those responsible for verification lack the time, tools, and incentive alignment to perform thorough checks.

Verification delegated to trust-based honor system

User Experience Flows

End-to-end user experiences across key personas: ITI Student, Industry Supervisor, ITI Coordinator, and District Officer.

ITI Student (Trainee)

ITI student undergoing mandatory in-plant training at industry partner locations. Typically 18-22 years old, using budget Android smartphones. Native Telugu speaker with varying digital literacy levels.

Daily Morning Check-in at Industry Site

Primary Flow
1

Opens attendance app at factory entrance

Student launches the app which displays a camera viewfinder with Telugu/English UI. The app initializes device sensors (camera, GPS, WiFi scanner) and loads enrolled face embeddings from encrypted local storage (SQLCipher). Pre-recorded Telugu audio prompts guide the student through the process.

Tech: Native Android (Kotlin) ¬∑ SQLCipher AES-256 ¬∑ Google TTS Telugu
2

Positions face within guide oval

MTCNN neural network detects face in camera frame. Quality gate runs: checks face detection confidence ‚â•90%, image sharpness ‚â•0.6 (Laplacian variance), and face coverage ‚â•80% of guide oval. Real-time feedback guides user to adjust position/lighting. Works in low-light industrial environments.

Tech: MTCNN (TFLite) ¬∑ OpenCV quality metrics ¬∑ 720p minimum capture
3

Liveness check (passive + active challenge)

Two-layer anti-spoofing: (1) Passive analysis runs LBP texture detection for photo attacks and moir√© pattern detection for screen replay. (2) Active challenge triggered 20% of time: random blink or head-turn verification using ML Kit face mesh. Defeats photo printouts, video replay, and basic masks. <300ms passive, <5s active.

Tech: Google ML Kit Face Detection ¬∑ LBP Texture Analyzer (TFLite) ¬∑ 99.5% photo attack detection
4

Multi-signal location capture

Silently captures GPS coordinates, visible WiFi BSSIDs (access point fingerprints), and cell tower IDs. Cross-validates signals to detect GPS spoofing‚Äîif GPS claims factory location but WiFi/cell signals don't match baseline, check-in is flagged. Handles indoor positioning in metal-roofed buildings where GPS is unreliable. 200m radius geo-fence with polygon support for irregular sites.

Tech: Android FusedLocationProvider ¬∑ WiFi BSSID scanning ¬∑ PostGIS polygon geo-fence ¬∑ Mock location detection
5

On-device face matching confirms identity

MobileFaceNet extracts 512-dimensional face embedding from captured image. Cosine similarity computed against enrolled embeddings stored locally. Match threshold 0.65 for instant approval (green checkmark). Lower confidence triggers server-side verification during sync. All processing on-device‚Äîbiometric data never leaves phone. <100ms on budget Snapdragon 4-series devices.

Tech: MobileFaceNet (TFLite INT8, 0.99MB) ¬∑ Cosine similarity ¬∑ 95% TAR @ 0.1% FAR ¬∑ GPU delegate acceleration
6

Encrypted local storage + background sync

Attendance record (timestamp, face embedding hash, location signals, device attestation) encrypted and stored locally. WorkManager schedules background sync when connectivity available‚Äîworks even if app is closed. Protobuf serialization minimizes bandwidth. Server performs secondary verification, anomaly detection, and dashboard update. CRDT-based conflict resolution handles multiple offline check-ins.

Tech: Android WorkManager ¬∑ Protobuf over HTTPS ¬∑ SQLCipher ¬∑ CRDT merge ¬∑ Play Integrity API attestation

Industry Supervisor

Factory floor supervisor overseeing 10-50 trainees. Busy with operational duties, views attendance admin as secondary. Uses personal smartphone, communicates primarily via WhatsApp.

Daily Attendance Review via Magic Link

Primary Flow
1

Receives automated WhatsApp summary at 10:00 AM

Scheduled job aggregates morning attendance by 10:00 AM and sends personalized WhatsApp message: "14 Present, 1 Absent, 1 Flagged. Tap to review." Message includes secure Magic Link with cryptographically-signed JWT token (48-hour expiry, single-use per session). No app download required‚Äîworks in any browser.

Tech: WhatsApp Business API ¬∑ Celery scheduled tasks ¬∑ JWT HS256 tokens ¬∑ Redis session store
2

Taps Magic Link - zero-login authentication

Link opens lightweight Progressive Web App in browser. JWT token validates supervisor identity without username/password. Token bound to supervisor's WhatsApp number (verified during onboarding). Session persists for 48 hours‚Äîcan close and reopen without re-authentication. Dramatically lowers adoption barrier for busy factory supervisors.

Tech: PWA (React) ¬∑ JWT validation ¬∑ HTTPS-only ¬∑ Device fingerprinting for security
3

Reviews flagged anomaly with explainable AI

Dashboard shows student photo, check-in time, and anomaly explanation in plain language: "Flagged because: Location 5km outside geo-fence + Device ID previously used by different student." SHAP-based explainability transforms ML model outputs into human-readable reasons. Map visualization shows expected vs. actual location. <5 high-priority alerts per day to prevent fatigue.

Tech: XGBoost anomaly detection ¬∑ SHAP explainability ¬∑ Leaflet.js maps ¬∑ PostGIS spatial queries
4

Single-tap "Approve" or "Dispute" decision

One-tap action records decision with supervisor ID, timestamp, and optional comment. Decision immediately written to append-only audit log (immutable). Approved records update student attendance status. Disputed records escalate to ITI Coordinator with supervisor's notes attached. All actions trigger real-time dashboard refresh for ITI staff.

Tech: FastAPI REST endpoints ¬∑ PostgreSQL append-only audit ¬∑ WebSocket real-time updates ¬∑ Event sourcing pattern
5

Auto-approval after 48h inactivity ("silence = consent")

If supervisor takes no action within 48 hours, record auto-approved by system. Prevents supervisor inaction from blocking student stipends or creating compliance gaps. Auto-approved records flagged in audit trail for monthly ITI Coordinator review. Reminder WhatsApp sent at 24h mark if pending items remain. Balances oversight need with practical adoption reality.

Tech: Celery beat scheduler ¬∑ PostgreSQL NOTIFY/LISTEN ¬∑ Exponential backoff reminders ¬∑ Audit flag: "AUTO_APPROVED"

ITI Coordinator

ITI staff managing in-plant training program across multiple industry sites. Primary user of analytical dashboards. Reports to Principal and District officials.

Monitor Attendance & Resolve Anomalies

Primary Flow
1

Opens web dashboard each morning

SSO login via OIDC (Keycloak) with role-based access control. Dashboard loads aggregate view showing all assigned industry sites with real-time attendance percentages. Color-coded heat map: green (‚â•90%), yellow (70-90%), red (<70%). Data refreshed via WebSocket‚Äîno manual reload needed. Sub-second query performance via TimescaleDB hypertables.

Tech: React Dashboard ¬∑ Keycloak OIDC SSO ¬∑ TimescaleDB time-series ¬∑ WebSocket real-time updates
2

Identifies site with low attendance (drill-down)

Click on red indicator reveals student-level breakdown. Shows individual attendance status, check-in timestamps, and anomaly flags. 7-day trend line identifies patterns (Monday dropoffs, post-holiday issues). Comparison against historical baseline highlights deviations. Can filter by trade, batch, or date range. Absent student list includes contact info for follow-up.

Tech: GraphQL flexible queries ¬∑ Chart.js visualizations ¬∑ PostgreSQL window functions ¬∑ Materialized views for aggregations
3

Reviews anomaly with explainable AI reasoning

Anomaly queue sorted by severity and age. Each flag includes plain-language explanation: "Flagged because: Location 5km outside geo-fence (contributes 60%) + Device ID previously used by Ravi Kumar (contributes 30%) + Check-in time 2hrs late (contributes 10%)." SHAP values quantify each factor's contribution. Evidence panel shows face photo, map with geo-fence overlay, and device history. Ensemble detector requires 2/3 signals to flag.

Tech: XGBoost + Isolation Forest ensemble ¬∑ SHAP TreeExplainer ¬∑ PostGIS ST_Contains ¬∑ Rule engine for critical patterns
4

Marks "Confirmed Fraud" or "Legitimate - Approved"

Decision recorded in immutable audit log with coordinator ID, timestamp, and reasoning. Confirmed fraud triggers: (1) Student notification, (2) Attendance record voided, (3) Alert to Industry HR, (4) Entry added to monthly fraud report. Legitimate approval updates student record and clears flag. All coordinator decisions fed to active learning pipeline‚Äîmodel retrains monthly using human-validated labels to improve precision.

Tech: Active learning feedback loop ¬∑ MLflow model versioning ¬∑ PostgreSQL audit log ¬∑ Pub/Sub event distribution
5

Generates monthly compliance report for District

One-click report generation with customizable date ranges. Outputs: (1) PDF executive summary with charts and KPIs, (2) Excel detailed breakdown by student/site/trade, (3) CSV raw data for District dashboard integration. Report includes: attendance rates, anomaly statistics, fraud cases, supervisor response times, stipend linkage recommendations. Auto-scheduled monthly report emailed to Principal and District Officer.

Tech: WeasyPrint PDF generation ¬∑ Pandas data processing ¬∑ Celery async tasks ¬∑ SFTP/API export to ITI Portal

District ITI Officer

Government official responsible for oversight of multiple ITIs in a district. Requires high-level analytics and compliance monitoring. Accesses system occasionally for oversight.

Review District-Wide Training Compliance

Primary Flow
1

Logs into district dashboard via state SSO

Authentication via AP State Government SSO portal (SAML 2.0 federation). Single sign-on means officers use existing government credentials‚Äîno separate password. Role-based access ensures District Officer only sees ITIs in their jurisdiction. Audit log records all login events for security compliance. Session timeout after 30 minutes of inactivity.

Tech: SAML 2.0 Federation ¬∑ Keycloak Identity Broker ¬∑ RBAC policies ¬∑ NIC-compliant audit logging
2

Views district-wide compliance dashboard

Executive dashboard shows KPIs across all ITIs: overall attendance rate, fraud incidents, supervisor engagement, stipend linkage status. Interactive heat map: click ITI to drill down, click trade to compare. Trend charts show month-over-month progress against targets. Comparison view benchmarks ITIs against district average. Pre-aggregated metrics via materialized views ensure instant page load even with 50+ ITIs.

Tech: React + D3.js visualizations ¬∑ PostgreSQL materialized views ¬∑ Redis cached aggregations ¬∑ GraphQL batched queries
3

Identifies underperforming ITI for intervention

Automated ranking highlights bottom performers. Drill-down reveals root cause analysis: Is it high absence? Supervisor non-engagement? Device issues? Industry partner problems? Shows ITI Principal and Coordinator contact info. Historical trend indicates if problem is chronic or recent. Anomaly score flags unusual patterns (sudden drop, weekday-specific issues). Comparison against similar ITIs (same trade mix, same district) provides context.

Tech: Statistical anomaly detection ¬∑ Percentile ranking ¬∑ Root cause decision tree ¬∑ Contact directory integration

Flags ITI for follow-up and tracks resolution

Progress tracker shows pending vs. resolved flags across district. Escalation workflow: unresolved items auto-escalate to State Director after 14 days. Monthly report aggregates all flagged items and resolutions for departmental review.
