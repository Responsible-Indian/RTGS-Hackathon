The Challenge

Where Every Boundary
Becomes a Battle

Andhra Pradesh's ambitious resurvey initiative confronts systemic obstacles that delay dispute-free land titles and burden survey teams with inefficiencies that compound at scale.

PROBLEM 01

Manual Parcel Delineation

No automated mechanism exists to extract land parcel boundaries from drone and satellite imagery. Each boundary must be traced by hand, consuming substantial time and creating backlogs across thousands of villages.

Time-Intensive
PROBLEM 02

Invisible Boundary Conflicts

Overlapping claims and boundary discrepancies remain hidden in the data until surveyors physically arrive on site. This late discovery transforms minor misalignments into protracted disputes.

High Risk
PROBLEM 03

Field Validation Bottleneck

Every single parcel requires physical verification by survey teams. Without pre-screening for anomalies, field resources are spread thin across routine checks instead of focusing on genuine conflicts.

Scalability Blocker
PROBLEM 04

Human Interpretation Variance

Different surveyors interpret the same aerial imagery differently. Without standardized automated extraction, boundary accuracy varies based on individual judgment, training, and fatigue levels.

Consistency Gap
PROBLEM 05

Record-Reality Disconnect

Historical textual land records frequently differ from actual ground measurements captured by modern GNSS technology. No systematic comparison exists to flag these discrepancies before they escalate into disputes.

Data Integrity
PROBLEM 06

Late-Stage Anomaly Discovery

Issues hiding in survey data—irregular shapes, impossible measurements, missing parcels—surface only during final validation stages. By then, resolution requires retracing the entire pipeline.

Process Inefficiency

The Compounding Cost of Manual Processes

These challenges don't exist in isolation. Manual delineation creates backlogs. Backlogs delay conflict detection. Delayed detection increases dispute complexity. Complexity consumes more field resources. The cycle perpetuates, scaling inefficiency alongside the state's ambitious resurvey goals.

Root Causes

Where the Pipeline Fractures

The problems persist because they emerge from structural gaps in how data flows, systems integrate, and decisions propagate through the resurvey workflow. Each fracture point amplifies inefficiency downstream.

BOTTLENECK 01

Fragmented Data Architecture

WebLand 1.0 textual records, drone imagery repositories, and CORS network outputs exist as isolated systems with no common query layer. Cross-referencing requires manual extraction and reconciliation between incompatible formats.

Conflicts remain invisible until data is manually assembled, often only during field verification
BOTTLENECK 02

Absent Intelligence Layer

No automated processing sits between raw geospatial data and human operators. Survey teams interface directly with unprocessed imagery and records, absorbing complexity that should be filtered, classified, and prioritized by algorithms.

Manual delineation persists as the default, and interpretation variance propagates unchecked
BOTTLENECK 03

Sequential Flow, No Feedback Loops

The resurvey pipeline operates as a one-way conveyor. Data moves linearly from capture to publication with no intermediate checkpoints that feed anomalies back upstream. Issues discovered late require restarting the entire sequence.

Anomalies compound through stages, reaching maximum resolution complexity at discovery
BOTTLENECK 04

Undifferentiated Workload Queue

Every parcel enters the same validation pipeline regardless of complexity or conflict probability. No risk scoring or anomaly pre-screening routes high-priority cases to limited field resources. Routine parcels consume capacity needed for genuine disputes.

Field validation becomes the choke point, scaling linearly with parcel count rather than risk
BOTTLENECK 05

Schema & Format Incompatibility

Textual records use tabular formats, imagery exists as raster tiles, boundaries as vector shapefiles, and GNSS data in specialized coordinate formats. No common semantic model exists to enable automated cross-system queries or reconciliation.

Automated comparison between records and reality remains technically infeasible without manual translation
BOTTLENECK 06

No Institutional Learning Loop

When field teams correct boundary errors or resolve disputes, that validated outcome data goes nowhere. No mechanism captures validation decisions to improve future extraction accuracy. Each survey cycle starts from the same baseline.

Accuracy remains static; the same errors recur across districts without systemic correction
