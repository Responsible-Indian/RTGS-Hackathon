Current Challenges

The Infrastructure
Blind Spot

Urban road networks are decaying faster than current manual workflows can monitor. Traditional methods lead to safety hazards, inefficiencies, and resource misalignment.

Human-Dependent Latency

Current inspection relies on manual field surveys. This process is inherently slow and inconsistent, leaving vast stretches of the road network unmonitored for extended periods while defects worsen.

Reactive vs. Proactive

Defects are often only identified after they have become critical hazards or triggered citizen complaints. This lag increases accident risk and exponentially raises repair costs.

The Accountability Void

Without an automated audit mechanism, there is no way to verify the quality of repairs. This leads to recurring defects, wasteful expenditure, and a lack of contractor accountability.

Data Blindness

Without automated severity assessment, minor cracks are treated with the same urgency as major craters. This prevents effective triage and resource allocation.

Operational Diagnostics

Where Roads Fall Through the System

Current road maintenance workflows face structural fragmentation. High-velocity data from modern sensors collides with legacy manual processes, creating specific failure modes in detection, assignment, and closure.

Manual Throttles Source Fragmentation Geo-Localization Drift Dedup Gaps PGRS Status Drift
Capacity Mismatch

Compute & Review Latency

Video ingestion from fleet dashcams creates a data volume that far outstrips manual validation capacity. Without an automated filter, thousands of hours of footage create a review backlog, meaning "real-time" video leads to "next-week" detection.

Identity Fragmentation

The Deduplication Gap

A single pothole detected by a passing bus, a CCTV camera, and a citizen complaint currently generates three separate work orders. The lack of spatiotemporal clustering and visual similarity matching results in redundant resource allocation and inflated defect counts.

Spatial Inconsistency

Geo-Localization Drift

GPS noise from moving buses, fixed CCTV offsets, and inaccurate citizen pins cause defects to "jump" across ward boundaries. Without map-matching logic to snap coordinates to road centerlines, repair teams are sent to incorrect locations, leading to "Unable to Locate" ticket closures.

Context-Free Triage

Severity Blindness

Absence of a severity rubric combining defect geometry, lane position, and traffic context means the repair queue remains unsorted. Planners cannot automatically distinguish high-risk corridors (near schools/hospitals) from cosmetic cracks, leading to reactive rather than risk-based scheduling.

Workflow Desynchronization

PGRS Status Drift

Status updates drift between physical reality and digital records due to manual data entry lags. A defect might be physically fixed, but remains "Open" in PGRS for days due to missing field evidence, preventing the AI system from closing the loop and validating the repair.

Latency Unblocked

Edge-First Intelligence

By moving detection to the bus (Edge AI), we bypass bandwidth bottlenecks. "Depot-Sync" transmits lightweight metadata instantly via 2G, while video evidence syncs over Wi-Fi, ensuring real-time visibility.

Duplicates Resolved

Smart Fusion

Whether detected by a bus, a CCTV camera, or a citizen, the system recognizes the same pothole. Spatiotemporal clustering and visual embeddings merge overlapping reports into a single, canonical defect record.

Locate Precisely

Map-Matching & Accuracy

Kalman-smoothed GPS combined with road centerline snapping ensures defects land on the correct segment. This eliminates "Unable to Locate" errors caused by urban canyon GPS drift.

Resources Optimized

Risk-Based Prioritization

Moving from FIFO to risk-based scheduling. The AI analyzes defect depth, road type, and proximity to schools/hospitals to assign a "Criticality Score," ensuring dangerous hazards are fixed first.

Accountability Enforced

Digital Trust Loop

Automated verification closes the accountability gap. Defect fingerprinting matches road texture in "before" and "after" photos, ensuring repairs meet quality standards before tickets are closed.

Statuses Aligned

Live PGRS Sync

Bi-directional updates keep physical reality and PGRS digital records in lockstep. Resilient, idempotent synchronization prevents status drift and ensures citizen transparency.

Architecture

Technical Design

A high-performance, offline-first architecture leveraging edge AI, hybrid connectivity, and systematic governance to scale detection across 11,495 buses.

Metadata Flow
Defect/Danger Data
Heavy Media (Video)
Sync / Feedback
Security Layer

01. INTELLIGENT EDGE

Buses leverage RPi 5 + INT8 NPU for 1 FPS inference. Only critical JSON metadata sends via cellular (MQTT); heavy video evidence waits for Depot Wi-Fi.

02. FUSION & SECURITY

YOLOv8 detections merge with citizen reports via spatiotemporal clustering. The pipeline is secured by a cross-cutting Governance Layer ensuring encryption, audit trails, and PII redaction.

03. CLOSED LOOP OPS

Validated defects feed back into the MLOps Pipeline for model retraining. Bi-directional sync with PGRS ensures field repairs, citizen status, and digital records remain perfectly aligned.
